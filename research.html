Research

Observations of exoplanet atmospheres in the optical and near-infrared.

I'm currently employed as a Gemini Science Fellow at the Gemini South Observatory in La Serena, Chile. Here, my research focuses on characterizing the atmospheres of ultra-hot exoplanets using a new high-resolution optical spectrograph, GHOST. As a member of the GHOST instrument team, I led the first atmospheric characterization of an exoplanet with this powerful new instrument.

Prior to starting at Gemini, I completed my PhD in Astronomy & Astrophysics at the University of Toronto. My PhD thesis focused on observations of exoplanet atmospheres using high-resolution optical and near-infrared observations from high-resolution ground-based telescopes.

I have published four first-author studies on the atmospheres of hot Jupiters and super-Earths, and am a Co-Investigator of an ongoing Gemini Large and Long Program (the ExoGemS survey) which aims to characterize more than 40 transiting exoplanet atmospheres over the next several years.
